ChatGPT has been out for a few years now. You've probably heard about it. Maybe you've even tried it once or twice, asked it something, got a response that was "fine but not quite right," and went back to doing things the old way.

That's the most common story. And the reason is almost never the tool — it's how you're using it.

Here's how to actually get value from ChatGPT as a small business owner.

## The #1 Mistake: Being Too Vague

Most people type something like "write me a marketing email" and get something generic. Then they think AI isn't useful.

The fix is context. ChatGPT knows nothing about your business unless you tell it. The more specific you are, the better the output.

**Weak prompt:** "Write me an email to get more reviews."

**Strong prompt:** "Write a friendly, short email asking happy customers to leave a Google review. I run a family-owned Italian restaurant in Denver called Rosario's. Our customers are mostly regulars aged 35-65. Tone should feel warm and personal, not corporate. Include a direct link placeholder."

Same tool. Completely different result.

## Five Ways to Use It This Week

### 1. Customer Emails and Follow-Ups
Stop staring at a blank screen. Describe the situation, paste in any context, and ask ChatGPT to draft it. You edit from there — which is 10x faster than writing from scratch.

### 2. Social Media Content
Give it your business info and ask for 10 Instagram caption ideas for the week. Pick the two you like. Done. What used to take an hour takes five minutes.

### 3. Answering Customer FAQs
Ask ChatGPT to write answers to your most common customer questions. Use those answers on your website, in emails, and on social. Once written, they're evergreen.

### 4. Creating SOPs (Standard Operating Procedures)
Describe how something works in your business out loud — like you're explaining it to a new employee. Paste that rough description into ChatGPT and ask it to format it as a clear, step-by-step SOP. Now you have documentation.

### 5. Brainstorming
Stuck on a problem? Describe it to ChatGPT like you'd explain it to a smart friend. Ask for 10 ideas. Most will be useless. Two or three will spark something real.

## A Simple System to Build the Habit

The hardest part isn't learning ChatGPT. It's remembering to use it.

Here's a simple rule: every time you're about to write something longer than three sentences, open ChatGPT first. Describe what you need. Get a draft. Edit it. This single habit will save you 30-60 minutes a week within the first month.

## What ChatGPT Can't Do

It can't know your specific business context unless you tell it every time (or use a tool like custom GPTs or Claude Projects to save that context). It also makes things up sometimes — don't use it for facts, legal advice, or financial calculations without double-checking.

Use it as a capable first-draft generator and thinking partner. Don't treat it as an oracle.
